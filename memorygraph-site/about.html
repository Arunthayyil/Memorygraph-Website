The Person Behind the Lens

Arun T
Chandran

Photographer · Filmmaker · Thrissur, Kerala
"I don't direct moments. I wait for them, recognize them, and preserve them before they vanish."

I grew up in Kerala — a place where weddings are louder, more emotional, more sacred, and more visually rich than almost anywhere else in the world. I watched my own family's celebrations unfold in clouds of jasmine and lamplight, and somewhere along the way, I knew I wanted to keep those moments from disappearing.

Memorygraph is what came of that. A small studio in Thrissur, run with intention. Documentary in spirit. Cinematic in craft. A handful of weddings a year — by choice — so I never become a photographer just going through the motions.

My Philosophy

Photography is just
listening
with a camera.

The best wedding photographs don't happen because a photographer gave perfect directions. They happen when a photographer disappears into the room, reads the energy, and is present in exactly the right moment.

I've spent years learning to be invisible — not absent, but unobtrusive. To blend into a celebration so completely that people forget I'm there. That's when the real moments surface — and those are always the ones that mean the most, years later.

01
Presence Over Direction

I rarely ask couples to pose. The real photographs come from real moments — and there are always more of them than people think.

02
Light as Language

Kerala's golden hour is unlike anywhere else in the world. I plan every wedding around it — and I shoot in the mornings most photographers sleep through.

03
Editing as Storytelling

Post-production isn't correction — it's the final act of the story. Warm, cinematic, restrained. Designed to age beautifully.

My Journey

How I got here.

The Beginning · 2017
First Camera, First Frame

Picked up my first DSLR with no idea what I was doing. Spent six months photographing temple festivals around Thrissur — and slowly realized I wasn't just taking photos. I was archiving culture.

First Wedding · 2018
A Friend Asked, and Everything Changed

A friend asked me to shoot her wedding. I said yes nervously. Looking back at those photographs now, they were rough — but they were honest. That's when I knew this was the work.

Studio Founded · 2020
Memorygraph is Born

Founded Memorygraph Wedding Studio with one rule: only the work that I'm proud of, only the couples I genuinely connect with. The numbers stayed small. The work got better.

Cinematography · 2022
Adding Motion to Memory

Brought cinematography in-house. Started building wedding films like short documentaries — with a story arc, real voices, real silence. Not music videos. Films.

Today · 2025
Intentionally Selective

Twelve weddings a year. Each one approached as if it's my first. Every couple given the time, attention, and craft they paid for — and a little more.
