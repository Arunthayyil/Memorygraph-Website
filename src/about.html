The Person Behind the Lens

Arun T
Chandran

Photographer · Filmmaker · Thrissur, Kerala
"There's a moment at every wedding — usually when nobody's looking — where something true happens. That's the only frame I care about getting right."

I grew up watching weddings the way most kids watch films — completely absorbed, unable to look away. Kerala weddings aren't quiet. They're full of competing rituals, six generations in the same room, light that changes every twenty minutes, and emotions that nobody rehearses. I found that irresistible.

I picked up a camera in my early twenties with no formal training — just an itch to document. I shot temple festivals, monsoon mornings, street markets. Then a friend asked me to shoot her wedding, and something clicked. Not the shutter, but the realization that this was where those skills actually meant something. That was 2018. Memorygraph followed in 2020.

We are a small team, deliberately so. I lead every wedding personally, alongside people I trust completely. We're not trying to be the biggest studio in Kerala. We're trying to do work we're genuinely proud of — and to still be proud of it ten years from now.

Our Philosophy

Photography is just
listening
with a camera.

The best wedding photographs don't happen because a photographer gave perfect directions. They happen when a photographer disappears into the room, reads the energy, and is present in exactly the right moment.

We've spent years learning to be invisible — not absent, but unobtrusive. To blend into a celebration so completely that people forget we're there. That's when the real moments surface. And those are always the ones that mean the most, years later.

Kerala weddings carry a particular magic — the rituals, the golden light, the generations gathered in one space. We consider it a privilege to hold that in our hands.

Presence Over Direction

We rarely ask couples to pose. The real photographs come from real moments — and there are always more of them than people expect.

02
Light as Language

Kerala's golden hour is unlike anywhere else in the world. We plan every wedding around it — and we show up in the mornings most photographers sleep through.

03
Editing as Storytelling

Post-production isn't correction — it's the final act of the story. Warm, cinematic, restrained. Designed to age beautifully and stay that way.

How We Got Here

The story so far.

2017 · The Beginning
A Camera I Couldn't Really Use

Bought a secondhand DSLR with no idea what I was doing. Spent the next six months at every temple festival and cultural event I could find in Thrissur — shooting badly, watching carefully, slowly understanding the difference between a photograph and a snapshot.

2018 · First Wedding
A Friend Asked, and Everything Changed

A college friend asked me to shoot her wedding. I was terrified. The photographs were raw — but they were honest in a way that changed how I understood what this work could be. She still calls them her favorites from that day.

2020 · The Studio
The Year I Said No to Everything That Wasn't This

Founded Memorygraph with one operating principle: only the work we're proud of, only the couples we genuinely connect with. We turned down more weddings than we accepted in year one. The work got better almost immediately.

2022 · Cinema
Adding Motion to Memory

Brought cinematography in-house and started building wedding films the way we'd want to watch them — real story arcs, real silences, real voices. Not a highlight reel. A film. The first one we made, we watched back four times before delivering.

Now · 2026
Quietly Devoted

A small team. A focused list of weddings each year. Every couple given our full attention — from the first call to the last delivered image. We're not trying to grow bigger. We're trying to do this better.
